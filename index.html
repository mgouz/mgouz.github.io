Does Money Buy Happiness¶

Introduction¶

What makes a country happy? Is it money? Is it the people? Is it the government? In this project I aim to demonstrate the data science pipeline using the World Happiness Index and answer a question. Like the famously misattributed quote to John Lennon, "I want to grow up to be happy", many of us desire to be happy as we grow older. As we age, that usually brings with it financial freedom to travel or expend it on other ways for ourselves. Whatever the case, we should know what makes us happy before we decide to spend it.

That's where this dataset comes in! We're gonna look over a span of years to see if over time money has made people happier. For countries with an increase in GDP (Gross Domestic Product/Overview for how well the country is doing) did they report an increase score in happiness.

Along the way, we will also learn about the data science pipeline so lets begin!

Conclusion¶

Great! We finished! We can safely conclude that money does make you happier! At least from this dataset. As we can see from the line of best fit and the coefficients of our linear model, both are positive. With an expected increase of 2.2348 happiness points every year for the countries with increasing GDP.

Every year too, we see that happiness is expected to increase as GDP increases.

You can predict that as your money grows so will your happiness! Sadly, this is likely not the full reality as there are many other factors at play in the dataset such as freedom of choices, generosity, family, and many other things. This was just one factor in a large net of life and it is impossible to say that the secret to happiness is money. However, I think it is fair to say that it probably would not hurt your chances.
